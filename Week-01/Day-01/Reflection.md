# Day 1 Reflection

Today I began my networking journey by learning the basic idea of what a computer network is and why it matters. I now understand that networks are created to connect devices so they can share data, resources, and services. The examples from everyday tools like WhatsApp, Google Drive, and YouTube made it easier to see how important networking is in everyday life.

One of the most important lessons from today was understanding that data does not travel as one large piece, but rather as smaller units called packets. I also learned about the difference between end devices and intermediary devices, and I became more familiar with the major network types such as PAN, LAN, MAN, and WAN. These concepts helped me see how networks are organized and how they support communication on different scales.

The Packet Tracer activity was especially useful because it gave me a hands-on introduction to the interface and the basic structure of a network. I enjoyed exploring the workspace, identifying the main parts of the environment, and placing devices such as PCs, a switch, and a router. Seeing the theory translated into a practical exercise made the lesson feel more concrete and engaging.

Overall, Day 1 was a strong introduction to networking fundamentals. I feel more confident with the basic vocabulary and concepts, and I am excited to keep building on this foundation. My goal for the next lesson is to become more comfortable with networking terminology and to connect the ideas I learned today to real-world network behavior.
