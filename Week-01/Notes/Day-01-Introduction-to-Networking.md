# Key Concepts

## 1. Computer Networks

### Definition
A computer network is a collection of interconnected devices that communicate with one another to exchange data and share resources.
Those devices include: computers, smartphones, servers, smart TVs, etc.

### Why Do Networks Exist?
Networks allow information to move quickly and efficiently between devices.
Think about services you use every day:

* GitHub
* WhatsApp
* Google Drive
* YouTube
* Netflix
* Online banking

Every one of them depends on computer networks.

### A Real-World Analogy
Imagine a community's road system.

* Houses = Computers
* Roads = Network cables or wireless links
* Cars = Data packets
* Traffic intersections = Switches
* Highways = Internet backbone
* Traffic officers = Routers directing traffic

Just as roads move people and goods, networks move data.

### Types of Networks

| Type | Description               | Example                               |
| ---- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| PAN  | Personal Area Network     | Bluetooth between a phone and earbuds |
| LAN  | Local Area Network        | Home, school, office                  |
| MAN  | Metropolitan Area Network | City-wide network                     |
| WAN  | Wide Area Network         | Internet connecting countries         |

You'll hear these terms often.

### How Data Travels

Suppose you send a message:
"Good morning."<br>
The message isn't sent as one huge block.

Instead:

* Your device breaks it into smaller pieces called packets.
* Those packets travel across different network devices.
* They may take different paths.
* They arrive at the destination.
* The destination device puts the packets back together.
