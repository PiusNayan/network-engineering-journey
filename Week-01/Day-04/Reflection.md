# Reflection — Week 1 Day 4

Today marked the final session of Week 1 in my network engineering journey, focusing on **Communication Protocols and Protocol Suites**.

Before today, I knew that terms like IP, Ethernet, and DNS were connected to networking, but I had not fully appreciated how they function as a synchronized team. Learning about protocols as systems of rules defining syntax, semantics, synchronization, and error recovery gave me a much clearer framework for understanding how computer communication stays reliable across diverse platforms.

The concept of a protocol suite and protocol stack was particularly insightful. Realizing that no single protocol handles an entire transmission on its own helped connect all the pieces I learned throughout Week 1:
- DNS translates domain names so we know where to send data.
- IP handles the logical addressing and routing across global networks.
- Ethernet takes care of delivering frames locally between MAC addresses.
- ICMP provides diagnostic feedback when we ping a device to test reachability.

Learning how data is encapsulated as it moves down the stack—turning raw data into segments, packets, frames, and finally physical bits—gave me a complete mental model of network communication from top to bottom.

Finishing Day 4 completes my first week of structured learning. I feel confident in the foundational concepts of networking, hardware components, packet switching mechanisms, addressing, and protocol stacks. I am eager to apply these fundamentals as I move into Week 2!
