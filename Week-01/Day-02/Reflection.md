# Reflection - Week 1 Day 2

Today I built my first fully functional Local Area Network (LAN) in Cisco Packet Tracer. Before this lesson, I understood that connecting devices with cables was not enough for communication. Through this practical exercise, I confirmed that devices also require proper logical configuration, including valid IP addresses and the correct subnet mask.

I learned the difference between physical connectivity and logical connectivity and why both are necessary for successful communication. I also practiced using the `ping` command to verify connectivity between devices and interpreted the results instead of simply checking whether they worked.

The biggest lesson from today is that network engineers design, configure, test, and verify networks systematically rather than relying on guesswork. This practical exercise strengthened my confidence in building and testing a simple LAN.
